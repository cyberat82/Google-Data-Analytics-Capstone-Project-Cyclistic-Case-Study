About Company:

Cyclistic, established in 2016, offers a successful bike-sharing service with a fleet of 5,824 geotracked bicycles and 692 stations in Chicago. Customers can unlock bikes from any station and return them to any other station in the system. Cyclistic's marketing strategy has focused on general awareness and targeting broad consumer segments, using flexible pricing plans like single-ride passes, full-day passes, and annual memberships. Casual riders use single-ride or full-day passes, while annual members subscribe to Cyclistic memberships.

Cyclistic's finance analysts found that annual members are more profitable than casual riders. To boost future growth, Moreno, the marketing director, aims to convert casual riders into annual members. Moreno believes targeting casual riders, who are already familiar with Cyclistic, could be an effective marketing strategy. The goal is to develop marketing strategies to convert casual riders into annual members by understanding the differences between them, identifying reasons for casual riders to buy memberships, and examining how digital media can influence marketing tactics. Analyzing Cyclistic's historical bike trip data for trends will help achieve this goal.

Scenario:

As a junior data analyst at Cyclistic, a bike-share company in Chicago, my responsibility is to analyze the distinct usage patterns of casual riders and annual members. The marketing team's goal is to create a strategy for converting casual riders into annual members, using convincing data insights and polished visualisations to gain executive approval.

Business Task Statement:

How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

The primary business task is to analyze Cyclistic's historical 12 months bike trip data from 2021 to identify and understand the differences in bike usage patterns between annual members and casual riders. This analysis will inform targeted marketing strategies aimed at converting casual riders into annual members, ultimately increasing profitability and driving future growth for the company.

ASK:

Q: What is the problem you are trying to solve?

A: How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

Q: How can your insights drive business decisions?

A: Through examining the provided dataset, I can formulate suggestions regarding the distinct behaviours exhibited by the two types of riders.

PREPARE:

In order to address this question, I will analyze Cyclistic's historical bike trip data from all 12 months of 2021. The data is dependable, unbiased, and has been gathered by Cyclistic and stored in the company's database as monthly CSV files. Motivate International Inc. has provided the data under this license(https://ride.divvybikes.com/data-license-agreement). For this project, I saved the 12 corresponding CSV files on my local drive, then uploaded them to Google Cloud Storage and imported them into BigQuery.

PROCESS:

For merging, exploration and cleaning the data and visualisation, I utilized SQL on Google's BigQuery platform, Spreadsheets and Tableau. Here's a summary of my approach:

1. Created a dataset named 'bike_project' and imported the 12 CSV files as individual monthly tables within the dataset.

2. Merged all 12 monthly tables into a single table 'combined_dataset' containing all bike trip data from 2021. This merged table consists of 5,595,063 rows and 13 columns, each with their respective data types. Used UNION ALL statement for merging and at the same time removing duplicates, used ORDER BY to sort it by date in ascending order.
